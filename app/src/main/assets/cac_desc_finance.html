Known as D Block, this billet for internees was adjacent to an athletic field which is now covered by a green lawn you can see today. In May 1944 D Block was involved in an incident known as ‘The Riot.’ Five internees escaped from Lunghwa and after a dangerous journey eventually reached Chinese Nationalist territory.

 

In D Block guards were aggressively interrogating the roommate of one of the escapees when he bolted and ran out onto the athletic field. The guards caught him, tied him to a soccer goal post and began to beat him. The residents of D Block, led by a group of women, rushed out and physically pushing away the guards, rescued the man and brought him back into the building, hiding him in an attic.




After a tense standoff that lasted overnight, the guards were reinforced with regular Japanese Imperial Army soldiers and the man was re-apprehended and then transferred to another camp in Shanghai. Commandant Hayashi resigned and was replaced by a new commandant who revoked several camp privileges: use of stoves, closing of the Lunghwa school, ordering internees to take meals in their billets.
